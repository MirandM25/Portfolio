ALL IN ONE DIGITAL PLATFORM FOR QUAYSIDE

Quaynect is a centralized digital platform designed for residents of Quayside, a new waterfront community in Toronto. It brings together concierge services, community engagement, and shared resource booking into one system, reducing friction in daily tasks while supporting affordability, wellbeing, and long-term belonging.

Rather than focusing on single moments, Quaynect is designed to support residents over time, from move-in through sustained participation in community life.

1. Community Belonging Is Fragmented and Hard to Initiate

Residents often struggle to form connections in new buildings due to the lack of visible, low-pressure ways to discover activities or engage with others. As a result, social participation feels effortful and inconsistent, causing many residents to remain socially disconnected despite living in close proximity.

2. Essential Services Are Scattered Across Multiple Systems

Essential services such as billing, maintenance, deliveries, and general support are spread across multiple systems, creating confusion and unnecessary friction in daily routines. This fragmentation increases cognitive load and reduces residents' sense of ease, trust, and control in their living environment.

3. Affordability Pressures Limit Participation and Access

High living costs shape how residents engage with their community, often discouraging participation in events, amenities, or shared experiences that feel costly or exclusive. Without affordable, shared alternatives, community life becomes constrained by financial barriers rather than enabled through collective access.

“Downtown housing prices aren’t fair, especially for students … utilities can cost $80/month.”
“Open, versatile spaces where people can hang out without spending money are key.”
“I love when it [a space] feels designed for people to actually spend time … tables, benches, greenery, shade.”

After conducting semi-structured interviews, we synthesized key insights by organizing notes into affinity maps to identify recurring themes, tensions, and needs across participants.

KEY INSIGHTS

↗ 01.

Convenience

Simplifying daily tasks improves how residents experience their community more naturally.

↗ 02.

Belonging

Everyday interactions and shared interests bring residents together and strengthen community ties.

↗ 03.

Affordability

Shared resources and sustainable systems reduce daily costs while supporting long-term living.

Guided by these insights, this led us to ask

How might we design systems that not only reduce everyday friction, but also create economic support, shared responsibility, and a stronger sense of comfort and belonging within the neighbourhood?

In response, we developed Quaynect, a centralized service platform designed to simplify everyday tasks, support shared access to resources, and enable community belonging to develop gradually through repeated, low-pressure interactions.

LESSONS LEARNED

↗ 01.

User Research

Understanding the diverse needs of community residents through interviews and surveys was essential to designing a platform that truly serves their daily lives.

↗ 02.

Iterative Design

Frequent testing and iteration helped refine the interface and ensure features were intuitive and accessible for all user groups.

↗ 03.

Systems Thinking

Designing for long-term community engagement required thinking beyond individual features to understand how different services interconnect.
